Reader picks

That our readers love

  • The Alchemist

    The Alchemist follows the journey of a young Spaniard named Santiago as he tries to realize his personal legend. He dreams of visiting the Great Pyramids of Egypt and finding a great treasure. He risks it all for his dream and embarks on an epic adventure for his treasure. He faces many hardships along his path and each step leads him closer to his treasure and self-realization. The Alchemist is a call to action to take risks and follow your dreams.

    Paulo Coelho

  • Awaken the Giant Within

    Tony Robbins has become a giant in our culture. Some people scoff when his name is mentioned. Regardless, Awaken the Giant Within is a book filled with the life experience and wisdom of Tony. It goes over subjects such as motivation and behavior. It asks you to consider what you want from your life and to create a plan on how to get there.

    Tony Robinson

  • Brave New World

    Brave New World is set in an alternate technocratic universe. Every aspect of society is controlled by technocratic overlords who control what people do and think. The terrifying part is the general population is oblivious to the manipulation and even welcomes it because they are so comfortable. Brave New World is a cautionary tale about the misuse of technologies in the service of societal control.

    Aldous Huxley

  • Inner Engineering: A Yogi's Guide to Joy

    Inner Engineering is a book which asks you to consider life from a different angle. Who is responsible for your experience of life? What are you really? These questions and more guide you into a deeper understanding of life. It suggests that you and only you are responsible for anything you are experiencing, be it pain or joy. If this is true, wouldn't you choose to create joy instead of pain in your life?

    Sadhguru

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and Practicum's library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 visitors each month. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and making use of our ample online resources.

The library gets its name from Joan Feynman, an American astrophysicist and the first woman to be elected as an officer of the American Geophysical Union.
